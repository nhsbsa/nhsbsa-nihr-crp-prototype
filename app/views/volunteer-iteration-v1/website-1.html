About the study

The END-RNP study wants to understand why some people keep getting neck pain, even after they recover. Researchers will follow people with recurrent neck pain over time to see how movement, muscle activity, psychosocial and daily life factors (like sleep or stress) might predict if neck pain comes back.

To answer this question the team needs about 300 people with recurrent episodes of neck pain to take part in the study.

Who can participate in the study:

You might be able to participate in this study if:

  • aged between 18 and 70 years.
  • experience of two or more episodes of neck pain during the previous 12 months.
  • now neck-pain free.

Who cannnot participate in the study:

You might not be able to participate in this study if:

  • you have a previous shoulder or spinal surgery
  • you have a history of neck trauma e.g. whiplash injury
  • you have any neurological or cardiovascular disorders
  • you have rheumatic joint disease
  • you are currently pregnant
  • you are currently receiving treatment of neck pain from the NHS
  • you have been advised by a doctor not to perform vigorous or exhaustive activity for medical reasons
